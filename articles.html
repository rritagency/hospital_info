Article 3

"Air pollution, notably prevalent in countries like Bangladesh, is increasingly linked to rising diabetes rates worldwide. Particulate matter (PM), nitrogen dioxide (NO2), sulphur dioxide (SO2), and ozone (O3) emitted from various sources are key pollutants associated with diabetes risk. Mechanisms such as inflammation, oxidative stress, alteration of adipokine levels, and endocrine disruption are proposed to explain this association. Epidemiological studies support the link between air pollution exposure and diabetes risk. While further research is needed, reducing air pollution could potentially mitigate diabetes risk, highlighting the importance of environmental policies promoting clean energy sources. The insights are shared by an Associate Professor at the Department of Endocrinology & Metabolism, Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujib Medical University (BSMMU)."

Article 7

"A study conducted in Bangladesh reveals a significant association between smokeless tobacco (SLT) consumption and Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD). Led by Dr. Mohammad Habibur Rahman Sarker, the research involved 872 adults and highlighted SLT's prevalence among certain demographics, including older adults, housewives, farmers, and those with lower education and income levels. The study underscores the need for education and awareness to combat SLT use and advocates for its inclusion in tobacco control strategies, given its cultural significance in countries like Bangladesh. Urgent measures are called for to address this overlooked aspect of tobacco control and mitigate the risks associated with CKD.""
